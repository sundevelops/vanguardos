## 2. Concrete improvements (3; min 3 required)

1. **Hero now shows the five tactile deliverables you keep, not an abstract process.** The hero promise was "in 5 days" plus a verb-only Day 1/3/5 rhythm; the new checked-pill row spells out the five finished assets a buyer walks away owning. *Why it converts:* this is exactly the SKILL's standing goal ("make the outcome tactile: brand, asset library, live landing page, high-impact offer, complete funnel, by Day 5"). Concrete nouns the reader can picture owning beat process verbs for desire, and the five pills make the "5 deliverables in 5 days" claim self-evident above the fold. The verb-by-verb process was redundant with the dedicated 5-Day Journey section below, so swapping (not stacking) keeps the hero from growing taller. Activating the dead `day5Outcomes` array also removes a maintenance trap.
2. **Offer rich-result eligibility strengthened with return policy + price validity.** Google's merchant/product structured-data guidance flags a missing `hasMerchantReturnPolicy` and `priceValidUntil`; adding both makes the Offer eligible for richer treatment and the return-policy annotation in results. *Why it converts/helps SEO:* the policy is encoded exactly as the locked guarantee reads (30 days, free return, no conditions), so it is honest, and a visible "30-day returns" signal in search results lowers click-risk for a cold shopper.
3. **Brand entity established via Organization schema.** A single-product page with no Organization node leaves the brand unresolved to crawlers. *Why it helps SEO:* the Organization entity (with logo and contact) supports brand knowledge-panel/logo treatment and ties the Product, FAQ, and site to one publisher, improving entity confidence with zero rendered-copy or price change.
