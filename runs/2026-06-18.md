## 2. Concrete improvements (8; min 3 required)

1. **Core-vs-bonus split is now unmistakable.** The 5-Day system gets its own gold, bordered, oversized card labelled "The core system"; the other six sit below a "Then six bonuses, free" divider, each with a gold "Bonus" pill. *Why:* the top-priority directive — a skimmer now parses one product + a bonus pile in under a second, which is exactly how value-stack offers convert. It reframes six line items from "more stuff to evaluate" into "free extras," lowering perceived decision cost.

2. **The $811 → $129 gap is shown as a ledger, not a sticker.** The value box now itemizes system $199 + bonuses $612 = $811 struck through, then $129. *Why:* breaking the discount into "the system" vs "the bonuses" makes the magnitude legible and credible instead of a bare before/after, and credible value math is what justifies the price.

3. **The killer honest hook: "$129 is less than the core system is worth on its own."** *Why:* 129 < 199 is literally true (verified), and it lands the bonus pile-on hard — the buyer feels the entire $612 bonus stack is free without a single hype word or fake-scarcity device. On-voice: premium, plain, calm.

4. **CTA standardized to the hard-locked control across hero + sticky + modal.** *Why:* the lock pins the CTA to "Get The Launchpad, $129"; the page had drifted (hero lower-case, sticky on an alternate, modal on a wrong count). One consistent primary CTA removes mixed messaging and satisfies the lock. (This is the run's logged CTA test — see §3.)

5. **Above-the-fold bonus seed.** Hero trust strip now reads "$811 value, six bonuses included," planting the core-plus-bonuses idea before the reader reaches the stack. *Why:* primes the frame so the offer section confirms an expectation rather than introducing it cold.

6. **Final-CTA reinforcement.** The closing paragraph now restates "core system plus six bonuses, $811 of value" right before "$129, once." *Why:* repeats the value anchor at the decision point, the highest-intent moment on the page.

7. **Removed an inaccurate "40 systems" claim** from the specimen modal. *Why:* it contradicted the "seven systems / one core + six bonuses" story and read as if $129 buys 40 products; replacing it with the control CTA keeps the offer honest and consistent.

8. **Guarantee eyebrow corrected to "The Ship-First Guarantee."** *Why:* brand-accuracy lock — the refund has one name, and the eyebrow now matches the body and the FAQ.
